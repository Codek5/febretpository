TUTORIAL STEP 1

After you login (you can use the automatic random name or specify one) you will be presented with a default view, consisting only of the bay model.

Right now you can:

  • Click and move the mouse to change viewpoint position
  • Shift + Click and move the mouse to change viewpoint zoom
  • Ctrl + Click and move the mouse to change viewpoint offset

When changing your viewpoint, a red indicator plane is shown. The indicator plane will stay on screen and will give you a hint on how you viewpoint is changing. When you release the mouse button, the indicator plane be substituted by the actual updated view. This technique is used instead of real-time updates of the model to achieve good reaction times even on complex, data-rich visualizations, and on slower machines.

Use the Map Panel (on the top-right corner) to select the visualized region, and to specify some parameters of the model. Just try and play with them to see what their function is!

Bring the mouse to the right of the screen to show the layers panel. Here you can add one or more data layers to the bay model. Add a layer, then select it to change its parameters.

When a layer is selected, a yellowish region will show up in the map panel. This is the region of interest for the selected layer. Shift-click and drag the mouse on the map panel to change it.

Now that you have a view with some data, you can play with the timeline to see how data changes over different time points. To better see how a view changes press ctrl, shift, left mouse button and drag the mouse horizontally. This feature allows you to easily compare the current view with its latest version and can be used whenever an update of the view or of the viewpoint is performed. Just try ctrl-shift-click and drag the view from time to time, to see its effect.

On the left side of the screen you find the workspace panel. Here you can publish your view and see at views published by other users. To send your current view to the workspace just click on the Add to Workspace button. Other users can now connect to your view by clicking on it and selecting the Get option.

Local cooperative configuration is determined by two simple options: Camera Update Settings and View Update Setting. Each of this options can assume three separate values that determine how updates are exchanged between clients: Input, Output and Input-Output. For a better explanation on how cooperation works on QbViz look HERE. The default cooperation setting on all clients enables collaboration only on view modification, while the viewpoint can be decided independently by each client.
