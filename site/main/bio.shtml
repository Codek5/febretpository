Why I Do What I Do



Visualization is playing an increasingly important role in the analysis of complex phenomena and simulations. As a research assistant at the UIC Electronic Visualization Lab (EVL), my main goals involve building tools that let researchers access and manipulate data in their own domains, using new and more efficient methodologies. This work does not simply involve the technical knowledge needed to create graphical applications. It also includes the ability to understand users and their own domain. Requirements vary across different people, and these requirements become more or less important depending on the target of our work: while the computational power of software tools is the main concern of domain experts, offering an engaging usage experience is the most important issue when building software for a more general audience, i.e. museum visitors. My passion for videogame design and development, along with my previous experience as a professional game developer, helps me in capturing those different concerns, and creating applications that try to offer not just functionality, but also an enjoyable interaction experience.

In this sense, the Electronic Visualization Lab offers the perfect environment to cultivate my interests. The new, cutting edge technology and software developed here is potentially targeting a very broad audience: from scientists working in national laboratories to museum visitors, from instructors interested in new learning technologies to casual videogame players.
